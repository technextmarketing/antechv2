03 / Assembly Process

10-step build workflow.

From gerber review to ESD-safe delivery — every build follows the same documented, traceable process.

01
DFM Review
Gerbers, BOM, and centroid reviewed for footprint issues, pad violations, and solder mask slivers before any material is committed.
02
IQC & Kitting
Incoming components inspected against BOM spec. Kitted per build job to prevent mix-up. Date codes verified and counterfeit screening applied.
03
Stencil & Paste Print
Laser-cut stainless stencil. RoHS-compliant solder paste applied with automated printer at controlled temperature and humidity.
04
SPI
Solder Paste Inspection measures volume, area, and height of every paste deposit before placement — catching bridging and insufficient paste early.
05
SMT Placement
High-speed pick-and-place with vision alignment. First article verified by engineer before full-run approval is issued.
06
Reflow Soldering
Profiled nitrogen reflow for oxidation-free joints. KIC thermal profiling confirms peak temperature and time-above-liquidus per IPC J-STD.
07
AOI Inspection
Automated Optical Inspection checks component presence, polarity, skew, solder bridging, and tombstoning after reflow with full traceability records.
08
THT & Wave
Through-hole insertion followed by selective or wave soldering. Flux application and topside temperature controlled per IPC-A-610 acceptance criteria.
09
QC & Testing
X-ray for BGA hidden joints, flying-probe or ICT electrical test, and functional test per customer-agreed test plan. Full test records issued.
10
Pack & Deliver
ESD-safe packaging, CoC and test records included. Same-country delivery to your facility — no customs clearance, no delays, no surprises.
04 / Quality Control

Six checkpoints. Zero escapes.

Every build passes through six independent inspection stages — each with documented records you can reference against your IPC audit requirements.

01

IQC — Incoming Quality Control

All components and bare boards inspected on receipt. Date codes, quantity, and package marking verified before kitting. Counterfeit screening available on request.

02

SPI — Solder Paste Inspection

3D solder paste volume, area, and offset measured at every deposit after printing. Boards failing SPI are re-printed — not sent to placement.

03

First Article Inspection

After placement, the first board of every job is manually verified by an engineer before the full run is approved. Component identity, polarity, and position confirmed against BOM.

04

AOI — Automated Optical Inspection

Post-reflow AOI detects missing components, solder bridges, lifted leads, tombstoning, and polarity errors with photographic evidence logged per board.

05

X-Ray — Hidden Joint Inspection

BGA and other hidden-joint packages inspected by X-ray for voiding, cold joints, and bridging not visible to AOI. Sampling rate or 100% inspection per your spec.

06

Final QA & Shipment Inspection

IPC-A-610 visual workmanship check by a certified IPC-CIS. CoC, test records, and traveller documentation packaged with every shipment.

05 / Testing Services

Test coverage for every build class.

From simple AOI to full functional validation — we match test coverage to your product's risk profile and qualification requirements.

AOI Testing
Automated optical inspection on every board post-reflow. Detects placement errors, bridges, and opens with full image archive.
X-Ray Inspection
BGA solder joint analysis — voiding percentage, bridging, and cold joints in hidden packages verified to IPC-7095 criteria.
In-Circuit Test (ICT)
Bed-of-nails fixture tests component values, opens, shorts, and basic function. Ideal for mid-to-high volume production boards.
Flying Probe
Fixtureless electrical test for prototype and low-volume builds. Checks opens, shorts, and component values without a dedicated ICT fixture.
Functional Test (FCT)
Board powered up and exercised through its operating modes using customer-supplied or Antech-developed test jigs and scripts.
IC Programming
Microcontroller and FPGA device programming during assembly. Firmware version controlled and verified post-programming before shipment.
Environmental Stress
Burn-in, thermal cycling, and humidity exposure for accelerated life testing. Supported by our QRA laboratory for full qualification packages.
Conformal Coating
Acrylic, silicone, or polyurethane coating applied to customer spec for protection against humidity, corrosion, and vibration.

09 / FAQ

Common questions answered.

PCB manufacturing produces the bare board — copper traces, drilled holes, and surface finish. PCBA (Printed Circuit Board Assembly) is the process of mounting electronic components onto that bare board using solder — creating a functional circuit. Antech handles both: we can coordinate bare board fabrication and deliver a fully assembled, tested board ready for your product.

There is no minimum order quantity. We regularly assemble single prototype boards for engineering teams and scale to production volumes of thousands of units for EMS customers. Setup costs are quoted transparently — you decide whether a small run makes economic sense for your project.

For a complete quote we need: (1) Gerber files or ODB++ for the bare board, (2) Bill of Materials (BOM) with manufacturer part numbers and approved alternates, and (3) centroid / pick-and-place file for SMT placement. If you are sourcing components yourself (consigned), provide them kitted per BOM. If you need turnkey sourcing, the BOM alone is sufficient to start.

Prototype builds (consigned components, simple boards) typically run in 3–5 business days after DFM approval and parts receipt. Turnkey builds depend on component lead time. Production volumes are scheduled based on complexity and quantity — contact us for a confirmed lead time based on your specific build.

Yes. We support IPC-A-610 Class 2 (general electronics) and Class 3 (high-reliability: medical, automotive, defense) builds. Class 3 jobs receive 100% AOI, X-ray inspection on BGA packages, and full traceability documentation including component lot codes, solder paste batch, and reflow profile records. Conformal coating and potting are available for additional environmental protection.
